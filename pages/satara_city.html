Sajjangad Fort

About:

Sajjangad Fort is a historic hill fort located near Satara in Maharashtra, India. The name Sajjangad means “Fort of Good People".It is best known as the final resting place of Sant Ramdas Swami, the spiritual guru of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort holds great religious and historical importance. Situated about 12 km from Satara city, the fort is surrounded by scenic views of the Sahyadri mountains. Devotees visit the fort to pay homage to Sant Ramdas, and daily prayers, aarti, and free meals (prasad) are offered to visitors.

History:

Sajjangad Fort near Satara was built by the Bahamani rulers and later captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1663. He named it Sajjangad, meaning “Fort of Good People.” The fort became the residence of Sant Ramdas Swami, the spiritual guru of Shivaji Maharaj. After the saints death in 1681, his samadhi (tomb) was built there. Today, Sajjangad is a famous pilgrimage site and symbolizes Maratha history and spirituality.

13th–14th Century: Built by Bahamani rulers.
15th–16th Century: Controlled by Adil Shahi dynasty.
17th Century: Captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1663; became home of Sant Ramdas Swami.
18th–19th Century: Developed as a spiritual and pilgrimage site.
20th–21st Century: Preserved by Ramdas Swami Sansthan; visited by thousands of devotees.

Ajinkyatara Fort

About:

Ajinkyatara Fort is a historic fort located in Satara, Maharashtra, built around the 12th century by the Shilahara dynasty. Later, it was ruled by the Bahamanis, Adil Shahis, and then captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1673. The fort also served as the capital of the Maratha Empire during Shahu Maharaj’s reign. Situated about 3,300 feet high, it offers a beautiful view of Satara city and is known for its temples, water tanks, and trekking spots.

History:

Ajinkyatara Fort was built in the 12th century by the Shilahara dynasty. It later came under the Bahamanis and Adil Shahis, and was captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1673. The fort was briefly the capital during Shahu Maharaj’s reign and played an important role in Maratha defense and administration. Today, it is a popular historical and trekking site in Satara.

Here’s a century-wise summary of Ajinkyatara Fort: 12th Century: Built by the Shilahara dynasty.
15th–16th Century: Controlled by the Bahamanis and Adil Shahis.
17th Century: Captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1673; later briefly the Maratha capital under Shahu Maharaj.
18th–19th Century: Continued under Maratha rule, maintained as a strategic fort.
20th–21st Century: Preserved as a heritage site and trekking destination.

Kaas Plateau

About:

Kaas Plateau, also called the “Valley of Flowers of Maharashtra”, is located near Satara, Maharashtra at an altitude of about 1,200 meters in the Sahyadri hills. It is famous for its seasonal wildflowers, which bloom in monsoon (August–September), creating a vibrant carpet of colors.

History:

Kaas Plateau does not have a long historical background like forts, as it is primarily a natural and ecological site. It gained prominence in recent decades due to its unique biodiversity and seasonal wildflowers, especially during the monsoon season. The plateau has been recognized as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site for its endemic and rare plant species, attracting botanists, nature enthusiasts, and tourists from all over India.
